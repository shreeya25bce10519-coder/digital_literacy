# Case Study: Phishing Attack on a College Student

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trusted
entities — such as banks, universities, or popular websites — to trick
victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or
banking details.

## The Scenario
Riya, a 1st year college student, received an email appearing to be from
her university's IT department. The email stated that her student portal
account would be suspended within 24 hours unless she verified her
credentials immediately. It included an official-looking logo and a
urgent link to "re-activate" her account.

Without checking the sender's email address carefully, Riya clicked the
link and entered her username, password, and mobile number on what
appeared to be the college login page. Within hours, her account was
accessed by an unknown party, and her personal details were misused to
apply for a fake student loan.

## How It Typically Happens
1. Attacker crafts a fake but convincing email or message
2. Victim receives a message with urgency (e.g. "Act now or lose access")
3. Victim clicks a malicious link leading to a fake website
4. Victim enters personal credentials or OTP
5. Attacker captures the data and misuses it immediately

## Who Is Usually Targeted?
Students, first-time internet users, senior citizens, and anyone unfamiliar
with verifying digital communications are most commonly targeted.

## Consequences
- Unauthorised access to personal and academic accounts
- Financial loss through stolen banking credentials
- Identity theft and misuse of personal information
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital platforms

*Source: Fictional scenario based on commonly reported phishing patterns
in India (Reference: cybercrime.gov.in)*
